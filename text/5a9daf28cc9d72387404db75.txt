School Audits
(cid:190) Audits are performed in accordance with State
law, which requires OLA to audit all 24 public
school systems once over a six-year period. As
of January 2010, 18 reports have been issued.
(cid:190) The purpose of the audits is to evaluate the
effectiveness and efficiency of the school
system’s financial management practices in
safeguarding assets and its use of financial
resources.
(cid:190) Audit approach consists of 11 functional areas
previously approved by the Joint Audit
Committee, as required by State law.
(cid:190) When warranted, we used the work of other
auditors to avoid duplication of effort.

Overview
(cid:190) FY 08 operating expenditures of $775 million,
including payroll of $559 million.
(cid:190) Enrollment of approximately 49,900 students in
73 schools.
(cid:190) Report contains 33 findings and 21
recommendations in 10 of the 11 areas reviewed.
(cid:190) Internal control problems were noted in a number
of areas, such as controls over certain cash
receipts and payments to bus contractors.
(cid:190) Policies need to be developed or improved in
certain areas.
(cid:190) Certain operational improvements should be made
to improve effectiveness and efficiency.
(cid:190) Certain best practices were found to be in place in
several areas such as human resources and
payroll, facilities and cash management.

Key Findings – Internal Controls
Revenues and Accounts Receivable – (p.9 and 10)
(cid:131) Certain receipts were not always adequately
safeguarded or submitted timely for deposit.
Purchasing Card Program – (p.20)
(cid:131) The same employee requested and received all
cards.
(cid:131) No periodic evaluation of card limits or card use.
(cid:131) Supervisory review of transactions was not always
documented.
Information Technology – (p.35)
(cid:131) Access to computer resources was not
adequately restricted.
Transportation– (p.44)
(cid:131) Data entered into the system used to pay bus
contractors were not reviewed by an independent
employee.

Key Findings – Policies
Accounts Receivable – (p.10)
(cid:131) No formal debt collection policy was established
for unpaid billings (for activity such as facility
rental).
Fuel Cards – (p.21)
(cid:131) No policy was established governing the use of
System-issued fuel cards, including monitoring
and documentation requirements.
Equipment – (p.29)
(cid:131) Policies should be adopted governing
accountability and control of sensitive
equipment items (such as computers and other
electronics).

Key Findings – Other Issues
Federal Funds – (p.15)
(cid:131) All federal funds for Medicaid-subsidized
services were not obtained.
Information Technology – (pgs.32-35)
(cid:131) Long-term technology plan did not include
measures to evaluate success or prioritize
actions and costs.
(cid:131) The adequacy of IT technical services had not
been analyzed.
(cid:131) A formal IT comprehensive disaster recovery
plan was not adopted.

Key Findings – Other Issues
Transportation – (pgs.45-47)
(cid:131) System-owned fleet usage was not monitored.
(cid:131) Performance measures were not developed and
used to evaluate student transportation costs or
contractor performance.
Food Service – (pgs.53-54)
(cid:131) Student participation in meal programs was
significantly below other similarly sized school
systems and national averages.
Health Care Costs – (pg. 62)
(cid:131) HCPS did not verify the authenticity of health
care program participants and dependents, nor
audit health care claims paid.
