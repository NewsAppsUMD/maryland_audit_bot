Crime Scene DNA Reporting Established
(cid:190) Chapter 337, Laws of Maryland, 2008
(cid:131) Established new data reporting requirement
for DNA evidence collected at crime scenes.
(cid:131) Local law enforcement units required to
report, on a biennial basis, certain information
from the previous calendar year.
(cid:131) Also requires hospitals to report certain
information.
(cid:131) First report due is for calendar year 2009
activity.
(cid:131) OLA responsible for receiving data, compiling
and evaluating the data, and submitting a
summary report to the Governor and General
Assembly.

What Will Be Reported
(cid:190) Reporting requirements include:
(cid:131) Crimes for which DNA is routinely collected
(cid:131) Approximate number of DNA evidence samples
collected for the preceding year by category of
crime
(cid:131) Average time between DNA evidence collection
and analysis
(cid:131) Number of samples collected but not analyzed at
the end of the reporting period
(cid:131) Number of samples submitted to the Statewide
DNA database in the preceding year
(cid:131) DNA evidence collected by hospitals

Actions Taken to Date
(cid:190) OLA worked with its Counsel to ensure data to be
reported by law enforcement units met law’s
requirements.
(cid:190) Assisted Department of State Police in developing
regulations (COMAR 29.05.01.16) to more
explicitly define the actual reporting requirements.
For example, the regulations:
(cid:131) Defined DNA samples collected as those
samples collected and submitted to a lab
for analysis, and
(cid:131) Set reporting deadlines.
(cid:190) Coordinated with GOCCP to develop the reporting
form to be used by law enforcement units.
(cid:190) Attended presentation made to Police Chiefs’
Association on reporting requirements.

Ongoing Work
(cid:190) Audit plans and programs under development by
OLA. OLA anticipates the process will include:
(cid:131) Sending notifications and reminders to police
units,
(cid:131) Visiting selected police units and testing of
reported data, and
(cid:131) Evaluating the law’s implementation and
related processes and results.
(cid:190) Reporting to OLA will be done in coordination with
Governor’s Office of Crime Control and Prevention
(GOCCP).
(cid:190) Reporting timeframes:
(cid:131) Law enforcement units to file data by Jan 31,
(cid:131) GOCCP will provide compiled data to OLA by
Feb 28,
(cid:131) DHMH to report the number of hospital DNA
examinations in each county by April 1, and
(cid:131) OLA prepares summary report after April 1.
